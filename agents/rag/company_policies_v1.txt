[0] General Provisions

Purpose: To safeguard compliant company operations, information security, and employee rights; to enhance collaboration efficiency and customer trust.

Scope: This manual applies to all personnel working on company premises, remotely/hybrid, or conducting work on behalf of the company.

Priority: If this manual conflicts with laws and regulations, laws and regulations shall prevail; if this manual conflicts with departmental rules, this manual shall prevail.

────────────────────────────────

[1] Professional Ethics and Code of Conduct

Basic Requirements: Integrity, respect, punctuality, confidentiality, non-discrimination, non-harassment.

Conflicts of Interest: Relationships involving relatives/investments with suppliers, customers, or competitors must be reported to the Compliance Department within 5 business days.

Bribery and Gifts: Accepting cash or cash equivalents is prohibited; gifts exceeding <amount limit> must be registered and surrendered.

Business Entertainment: Prior approval required (see [23] Approval Matrix); expenses must be genuine with complete receipts; reimbursement within 2 business days after the event.

────────────────────────────────

[2] Anti-Harassment and Anti-Discrimination Policy

Zero Tolerance: Discrimination/harassment based on gender, age, race, region, religion, health status, etc. is strictly prohibited.

Complaint Channels: Submit anonymously to whistle@company.com or report to HR/Compliance Department.

Processing Principles: Confidentiality, legal compliance, timely investigation, prohibition of retaliation.

────────────────────────────────

[3] Working Hours and Overtime

Standard Hours: Daily <9:00-18:00>, lunch break <12:00-13:00>, weekends off/shift schedules per department policy.

Flexibility and Remote Work: Manager approval required with system registration; remote work requires daily check-in via <attendance system/IM>.

Overtime: Prior approval required; overtime compensation per national laws and company policy (compensatory leave/overtime pay).

────────────────────────────────

[4] Leave and Absence

Leave Types: Annual leave, sick leave, personal leave, maternity/paternity leave, marriage/bereavement leave, public holidays, etc.

Application: Annual leave requests must be submitted ≥3 business days in advance; sick leave exceeding 1 day requires medical certificate.

Absence: Unnotified absence is treated as unauthorized absence; consecutive unauthorized absence ≥3 days is considered serious misconduct.

────────────────────────────────

[5] Information Security and Data Protection (Applicable to All Employees)

Classification: Data is classified as 【Public/Internal/Confidential/Highly Confidential】; "Confidential+" data should not be shared externally by default.

Principles: Minimum privilege, need-to-know basis, encrypted transmission, retention as needed, regular destruction (see [15] Document Retention).

External Sharing: Do not share code, documents, customer information, logs, model weights, or keys externally without authorization.

Violation Examples: Exporting customer lists to personal email/cloud; committing code containing keys to public repositories; leaking unreleased features on social media.

────────────────────────────────

[6] IT and Equipment Usage (Including BYOD)

Equipment: Company equipment is for work use only; personal devices (BYOD) must have company MDM/antivirus and disk encryption installed.

Accounts: Personal accounts are for individual use only; shared accounts require compliance approval with audit enabled.

Passwords: ≥12 characters including uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters; change every 90 days; reusing passwords from external services is prohibited.

Software: Only install software from the IT-approved whitelist; pirated software, mining, and P2P downloads are prohibited.

────────────────────────────────

[7] Network and Access Control

VPN: Remote access to internal network/code repositories must use company VPN; access rights are revoked on the day of departure.

Cloud Resources: Creation, modification, and deletion of cloud resources must follow infrastructure change procedures (see [12] Change Management).

Log Auditing: Critical systems must have access logs and operation audits enabled; audit logs must be retained for a minimum of <180> days.

────────────────────────────────

[8] Confidentiality and Intellectual Property (IP)

Confidentiality Obligation: Confidentiality obligations apply during employment and for <2 years> after departure.

Ownership of Work: Code, documents, designs, models, patents, and other intellectual property created within the scope of employment belong to the company.

Open Source Compliance: External open-sourcing or use of strong Copyleft components like GPL/AGPL requires legal approval; third-party licenses must be registered (see [19] Open Source Policy).

────────────────────────────────

[9] External Communications and Media/Social Media

Spokesperson Authority: News, funding announcements, customer partnerships, security incidents, etc. may only be released by Brand/PR/Senior Management.

Social Media Conduct: Employees must not disclose unpublished information, impersonate company positions, or disparage customers/partners.

Public Speaking: Speaking on behalf of the company requires approval; materials involving customer or technical details require legal review.

────────────────────────────────

[10] Customer and Supplier Management

Contracts: All external contracts require legal review and signature by authorized signatories; private signing is prohibited.

Procurement: Purchases ≥<amount threshold> must follow procurement procedures; purchases ≥<higher threshold> require at least three quotes with records retained.

Anti-Fraud: Kickbacks, undisclosed related-party transactions, and fraudulent invoices are strictly prohibited.

────────────────────────────────

[11] Expense Reimbursement and Business Travel

Principles: Genuine, necessary, economical; approval before expenditure; legitimate and compliant receipts.

Travel: Flights/trains/hotels must be booked through designated platforms; class/standard per position level and policy.

Reimbursement: Submit within <30> days of expenditure; invoice details must match company information.

────────────────────────────────

[12] Change Management (Technical/Process)

Applicable To: Production environment changes, critical configuration updates, major process adjustments.

Requirements: Change request, rollback plan, impact assessment, approval records, execution during designated windows, post-change verification.

Emergency Changes: "Execute first, approve later" is permitted but documentation must be completed within 2 business days.

────────────────────────────────

[13] Security Incident and Violation Reporting

Definition: Data breaches, account theft, virus attacks, system outages, compliance violations, etc.

Response: Report to incident@company.com and IM group within 1 hour of discovery; major incidents must be reported to Legal and Management within 2 hours.

Resolution: Tiered response, root cause analysis and remediation, customer notification (if required), review and improvement.

────────────────────────────────

[14] Physical Security and Visitors

Access Cards: Lending or borrowing access cards is prohibited; report loss immediately.

Visitors: Registration, visitor badge, and full-time escort required; unauthorized entry to server rooms/R&D restricted areas is prohibited.

Hardware: Equipment, ID cards, and confidential documents must be locked when leaving; clear/lock screen when leaving the office area.

────────────────────────────────

[15] Document Retention and Destruction

Retention Periods:

Contracts/Financial: ≥10 years

Personnel Records: ≥5 years after departure

Logs/Audits: ≥180 days (critical systems ≥1 year)

Destruction: Shredding/secure wiping after approval upon expiration; unauthorized disposal of confidential documents/hard drives is prohibited.

────────────────────────────────

[16] Performance, Promotion, and Rewards/Discipline

Performance: Quarterly/semi-annual evaluations including goal achievement, behavioral standards, team collaboration, and customer satisfaction.

Promotion: Open criteria, structured interviews, results publication (with sensitive information redacted).

Disciplinary Action: Verbal warning → Written warning → Recorded penalty → Demotion/pay reduction → Termination; severity adjusted per circumstances.

────────────────────────────────

[17] Health and Safety (EHS)

Office: Follow fire safety, electrical usage, first aid, and emergency evacuation guidelines; participate in annual drills.

Work from Home: Ergonomic chair and desk recommended; follow the 20-20-20 screen break rule.

Mental Health: Anonymous psychological counseling available; flexible arrangements encouraged to avoid excessive overtime.

────────────────────────────────

[18] Data Classification and External Transmission Examples

Public: Content published on the website, public recruitment information.

Internal: Unpublished internal notices, process documents.

Confidential: Customer lists, financial statements, unreleased feature designs, model weights, API keys.

Highly Confidential: Mergers & acquisitions, funding, bid floor prices, root keys.

External Transmission Requirements: Encrypt Confidential+ data (PGP/company SFTP/controlled share links with time and access limits); mark email subjects with 【Confidential】.

────────────────────────────────

[19] Open Source and Third-Party Compliance

Import: Evaluate licenses (MIT/Apache2 preferred; GPL/AGPL require legal review); retain copyright and license files.

Contribution: External code submissions require direct supervisor and legal approval; do not upload company confidential information.

Models/Data: Third-party datasets/models must be from legitimate sources and comply with terms (usage restrictions/redistribution restrictions).

────────────────────────────────

[20] Personnel Changes and Departure Handover

Departure Process: Submit application → Asset inventory → Knowledge transfer → Access revocation → Exit interview → Issue certificate.

Assets: Return laptop, access card, ID card, test equipment; delete company data from personal accounts.

Confidentiality: Taking code, documents, customer information, and data samples after departure is prohibited.

────────────────────────────────

[21] Training and Mandatory Courses

Onboarding Required: Information security, anti-bribery, anti-harassment, data compliance, confidentiality agreement.

Annual Refresher: Security awareness, emergency drills, compliance updates.

Passing Standards: Complete online courses and tests; retraining required if failed.

────────────────────────────────

[22] Exceptions and Exemptions

When business needs require, employees may submit an "Exception Request" stating risks, time limits, and compensating control measures.

Exception approvals are valid for no more than <90> days; automatically expire upon expiration and require re-evaluation.

────────────────────────────────

[23] Approval Matrix (Example, adjust per company)

Expense Reimbursement: Department Manager ≤<$5k>; Finance Director ≤<$50k>; higher amounts require General Manager approval.

Contract Signing: After legal review, signature by authorized signatory (see internal announcement for list).

Changes: Production-level changes require Change Team + Operations Head approval; emergency changes must be approved within 2 days.

────────────────────────────────

[24] Disciplinary Action and Appeals

Process: Fact investigation → Notification and defense → Decision → Record.

Appeal: Employees may appeal within 5 business days; review by Compliance/Disciplinary Committee.
